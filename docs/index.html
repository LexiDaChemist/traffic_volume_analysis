Interstate Traffic Volume Analysis

This project analyzes hourly interstate traffic volume using the Metro Interstate Traffic Volume dataset to uncover repeatable patterns in human mobility. The figures show how demand changes across the day, differs between weekdays and weekends, and responds to weather conditions. The emphasis is interpretability which is turning raw data into visual evidence that supports potential infrastructure planning and optimal decision-making outcomes.

Figures

Real-World Applications

The above results indicate how large systems reveal patterns when observed over time. By examining hourly changes, we can see how human behavior; such as commuting schedules and daily routines, creates predictable increases and decreases in roadway demand. These peaks highlight moments when infrastructure experiences the greatest pressure, making them especially important for analysis. When weather conditions are added, the data becomes more meaningful: high traffic combined with poor weather increases both risk and system stress. For future scientists and researchers, this type of analysis demonstrates how real-world data can be transformed into evidence that informs planning and decision-making; for example, in development of infrastructure.

Discussion

Overall, the figures show that traffic volume changes in consistent and understandable ways over time. On weekdays, traffic tends to peak during common commuting hours, while weekend traffic is spread out more evenly and usually increases later in the day. Weather adds useful information, but the time of day and whether it is a weekday or weekend have a stronger influence on traffic levels. This project focuses on making patterns easy to see by using simple summaries and clear visualizations instead of complex models. Although the results apply to this specific interstate, they demonstrate how repeated patterns in data can be used to understand normal behavior and support planning decisions that rely on predictable trends.
